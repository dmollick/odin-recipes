Aunt Carol's Famous Sugar Cookies

Description:

The year was 1887 when great great grandma Aunt Carol developed her famous sugar cookies in a test kitchen just outside of Columbia, Missouri. Aunt Carol worked day and night developing these cookies for 16 years until one day she realized she had finally perfected the them. Legend has it, old Sister Stink passed away after eating so many of Aunt Carol's famous cookies.

Aunt Carol always said, "The secret is in the icing."

Ingredients:

Cookie Base
  • 1 1/2 cup Powdered Sugar
  • 1 cup softened butter
  • 1 egg
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/2 tsp almond extract
  • 2 1/2 cups flour
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1 tsp cream of tartar
Icing
  • 2-3 tbsp Crisco
  • A lot of powdered sugar
  • Splash of water
  • 1/2 tbsp vanilla extract
  • 1/2 tbsp almond extract

Steps:

  1. Mix sugar and butter.
  2. Add egg and flavorings (vanilla/almond extract).
  3. Stir in dry ingredients.
  4. Refrigerate 2-3 hours if possible.
  5. Heat oven to 375 degrees fahrenheit.
  6. Roll out dough, cut cookies, and place on a greased baking sheet or parchment paper.
  7. Bake 7-8 minutes.
  8. Let cool and ice the cookies.
